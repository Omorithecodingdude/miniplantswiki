Lavender

Lavandula

Lavender is a flowering plant in the mint family, known for its fragrant purple flowers and silvery-green foliage. Native to the Mediterranean region, it's prized for its calming scent and versatile uses in aromatherapy, cooking, and decoration.

  • Ancient Romans used lavender for bathing, cooking, and scenting the air.
  • The name comes from the Latin "lavare" meaning "to wash" because it was used in baths.
  • There are over 45 species of lavender and hundreds of varieties.
  • Lavender honey is a premium product produced in the Mediterranean region.
  • Sunlight: Full sun (6+ hours daily)
  • Water: Drought-tolerant; water when soil is dry
  • Soil: Well-draining, slightly alkaline
  • Pruning: Cut back by 1/3 in early spring
  • Hardiness: Zones 5-9, depending on variety

Lily of the Valley

Convallaria majalis

Lily of the Valley is a sweetly scented woodland flowering plant native to Europe and Asia. Its delicate white bell-shaped flowers and rich green leaves make it a favorite for shady gardens and floral arrangements, though all parts of the plant are poisonous if ingested.

  • It's the national flower of Finland and the floral emblem of Yugoslavia.
  • In Christian legend, the flowers grew from the tears of Mary at the crucifixion.
  • The flower symbolizes humility and purity in Victorian flower language.
  • Despite its beauty, it's highly toxic to pets and humans if consumed.
  • Sunlight: Partial to full shade
  • Water: Keep soil consistently moist
  • Soil: Rich, well-draining humus
  • Propagation: Divide rhizomes in fall
  • Hardiness: Zones 2-7

Gladiolus

Gladiolus spp.

Gladiolus, also called sword lily, is a genus of perennial flowering plants known for their tall flower spikes and large, colorful blooms. Native to South Africa and Mediterranean Europe, they're popular as cut flowers and garden plants, with flowers in nearly every color.

  • The name comes from Latin "gladius" meaning sword, referring to the plant's sword-shaped leaves.
  • In Victorian flower language, gladiolus symbolizes strength of character.
  • The flower associated with 40th wedding anniversaries.
  • Some species have edible flowers and can be grown as food crops.
  • Sunlight: Full sun
  • Water: Keep soil moist but not waterlogged
  • Soil: Well-draining, fertile
  • Support: Stake tall varieties
  • Storage: Lift corms in colder zones (below 7)

White Egret Orchid

Habenaria radiata

The White Egret Orchid is a stunning terrestrial orchid native to Japan, Korea, and China. Its flowers resemble white egrets in flight, with fringed petals that give an appearance of feathers. This rare and protected species is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

  • Also called "Sagisō" in Japan, meaning "heron orchid."
  • The flower shape resembles the great egret (Ardea alba) in flight.
  • Listed as a rare and protected species in Japan.
  • Blooms for about 2-3 weeks in summer if properly cared for.
  • Sunlight: Bright indirect light
  • Water: Keep constantly moist during growth
  • Soil: Well-draining orchid mix with sphagnum
  • Dormancy: Reduce water after flowering
  • Humidity: High humidity preferred
